## COPY STRATEGY NOTES (For Designer/Developer Reference)

### Voice & Tone
- **Professional but approachable** — not "agentic" developer-speak (that's Postiz's lane)
- **Outcome-led** — every feature description leads with the user's result, not the feature itself
- **Specific over vague** — "19+ platforms" beats "multi-platform"; "27,800+ stars" beats "popular on GitHub"
- **Confident, not humble** — direct statements, not hedged language

### Headline Recommendation
**Use Variation A**: "All your social media. One smart dashboard."
- Shortest
- Most memorable
- Passes the 3-second scan test
- Doesn't over-promise (avoids "grow your business" cliché)

### CTA Strategy
- **3 CTA placements**: Hero → Mid-page → Bottom
- **Primary**: "Start Free" (not "Sign Up" — implies free value, not form-filling)
- **Always include**: "No Credit Card Required" under primary CTA
- **Secondary CTA**: Keeps visitor engaged if they're not ready to sign up

### Social Proof Priority Order
1. GitHub stars (27,800+) — community validation, open-source credibility
2. Platform count (19+) — specificity builds trust
3. Named testimonials with personas — personal trust
4. Logo bar — visual trust signal

### Mobile Considerations
- Hero headline should break at "All your social media." / "One smart dashboard."
- Trust signal bar scrolls horizontally on mobile
- CTA button: full-width on mobile, 280px max on desktop
- Comparison table: scroll-able on mobile or collapsed to key rows
