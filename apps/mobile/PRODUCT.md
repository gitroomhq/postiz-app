# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Postiz mobile is for social media operators, founders, creators, and small teams who need to check schedules, recover failed posts, connect channels, approve or adjust content, and compose from phone media while away from the desktop app.

## Product Purpose

Postiz mobile translates the existing Postiz scheduling and channel-management workflow into native mobile ergonomics. Success means a user can quickly see what is scheduled, understand which channels need attention, compose or draft a post, attach phone media, recover from failed publishing, and act on notifications without losing trust in the desktop product.

## Brand Personality

Restrained, operational, and confident. The app should feel like a serious publishing control room in the user's pocket: dense enough for real work, calm enough for repeated use, and selective with emphasis.

## Anti-references

Do not copy competitor colors, roundedness, illustration language, or marketing-page composition. Do not treat Dribbble concepts as product truth; they often hide loading, error, permission, retry, and approval states. Avoid oversized hero cards, decorative motion, overdecorated calendar rows, and modal-first ordinary flows.

## Design Principles

- Preserve Postiz identity: dark-first, compact surfaces, purple for primary action and selection, pink only for AI or high-emphasis moments.
- Lead with workflow state: scheduled, failed, draft, approval, reconnect, and notification recovery must be easier to scan than decorative content.
- Keep creation thumb-reachable: composing, adding media, changing channels, and scheduling should stay close to the bottom-hand workflow on mobile.
- Borrow workflow evidence, not layout: use production social-management apps for constraints, then apply Postiz's own visual vocabulary.
- Motion explains state changes: use short Reanimated transitions for skeletons, trays, validation, and route feedback, never choreography.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target readable contrast in dark and light modes, stable tap targets of at least 44px, visible selected/disabled states beyond color alone, reduced-motion-friendly transitions, and copy that tells users how to recover from errors.
